My Thoughts on the Near Future of AI

Apr 1, 2026 · 6 min read · Ivan Santongo

If you follow the loudest conversations online, the future of AI sounds like a disaster movie.

Either AI replaces most jobs, or it becomes fully autonomous and takes control of systems we can barely understand. The story keeps swinging between excitement and dread, and neither end of that swing feels particularly useful.

From where I sit, actually building an AI product, the future looks a lot more grounded than that.

I do not believe we are heading toward a world where AI becomes completely independent anytime soon. And I do not believe human involvement is disappearing from the equation. What I see instead is a long stretch of time where humans and AI work closely together.

Not replacement. Partnership.

AI Works With Humans, Not Instead of Them

Today's AI is genuinely impressive. It can write, generate images, assist with code, analyze information, and automate tasks that used to eat entire afternoons.

But there is one thing people consistently misunderstand.

AI does not actually think the way humans do. Most modern systems work by predicting patterns, generating outputs based on what they were trained on. In plain language, they are extraordinarily sophisticated pattern matchers. That makes them remarkably useful, but it also means they still need direction.

Human judgment still matters. Even the most advanced systems today rely on human prompts, human oversight, and human decisions to guide them toward anything useful.

Which is why the idea that AI will suddenly become fully independent in the near future feels unrealistic to me. Maybe that happens someday. But not soon.

The AGI Question

The term that keeps appearing in these conversations is AGI, Artificial General Intelligence. A system that can perform any intellectual task a human can, with the same flexibility and depth of reasoning.

Many researchers believe it will happen eventually. When is a different question entirely.

Some say within a decade. Others say many decades. And a lot of serious people refuse to make strong predictions at all.

A few perspectives worth noting:

Yann LeCun, Meta's Chief AI Scientist, has argued repeatedly that current AI is far from true human-level intelligence and that major breakthroughs are still required before anything resembling AGI shows up.

Andrew Ng has made a similar point that fears about superintelligent AI are misplaced right now, because today's systems are still narrow tools, not autonomous thinkers.

Demis Hassabis at DeepMind, while believing AI will become extremely powerful over time, has consistently emphasized that careful development and human oversight remain critical.

These are not pessimists. These are the people building the technology. And even they see today's AI as powerful but limited.

What We Are Actually Seeing Right Now

From 2024 through 2026, AI models have improved significantly, with better reasoning, stronger coding ability, improved multimodal systems, and tools that can handle complex workflows.

But if you look closely, most of those improvements have been incremental. Not revolutionary. Models are getting better, faster, and more capable. We have not crossed into a world where AI operates on its own.

The AI agents wave is a good example of this.

Systems like AutoGPT, Devin, and various autonomous coding tools showed real promise. They could follow instructions, break tasks into steps, and attempt to reach goals. But when given too much independence, they struggled. They made mistakes, lost context, and required correction. They worked best when operating inside structured workflows with a human in the loop.

That tells me something honest about where we actually are.

AI is extremely useful when working alongside people. It still needs guidance.

AI Is Changing Work - Not Eliminating It

The most visible impact right now is not replacement. It is productivity.

Software engineering is the clearest example. Building a complex product used to require large teams of dozens of developers hired to build, maintain, and iterate. Today, AI tools assist with coding, debugging, testing, documentation, and planning. Smaller teams can build more. A team that might have needed 20 or 30 people can operate effectively with far fewer, working closely with AI throughout.

But notice what actually changed. Developers did not disappear. Their output increased.

That same pattern is starting to show up across design, marketing, data analysis, customer support, and research. AI is helping people work faster, experiment more, and explore ideas that would have taken much longer to test. The role shifts from doing every task manually to guiding systems that assist with the work.

What I Have Seen as a Designer

I have experienced this shift directly.

Not long ago, I would have to purchase stock images, subscribe to asset libraries, or spend real money on design resources just to get the materials I needed. Today, I can generate images, brainstorm ideas faster, and experiment with visual directions in minutes instead of hours.

But even with all of that, AI is still a tool. It does not replace the judgment that makes a design decision good or bad. It removes friction. The human is still making the call.

The Real Shift: Human + AI

The future I find most realistic is not one where AI replaces people.

It is one where AI becomes part of how people work. Those who know how to use it well become significantly more productive. Where experts become more powerful because they can extend their capabilities with AI. Where smaller teams build things that used to require much larger organizations.

But humans stay in the picture. Judgment, creativity, and responsibility do not go away; they become more important, not less.

Final Thought

AI is one of the most powerful technologies we have seen in decades. It is changing how we work, how we create, how we build.

But the future is not a story of machines replacing people. At least not in any timeframe worth planning around.

The more honest version of the future is one where humans and AI work together, where the technology amplifies what people can do, rather than cutting them out of the process.

And that future is not coming. It is already here.
