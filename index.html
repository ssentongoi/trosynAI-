The Problem

Cloud AI fails first where reliability matters most.

The first market is in African operating environments, but the problem is broader: businesses cannot run core workflows on AI tools that depend on constant connectivity, easy cross-border data movement, and cloud-first pricing. The same constraints appear in Western markets through compliance requirements (GDPR, audit control, internal data policies), even where infrastructure is not the limiting factor.

📡
Connectivity cannot be a requirement
In the launch markets, internet shutdowns and poor connectivity make cloud AI unreliable at the exact moment teams need it. That same failure mode appears anywhere operations depend on unstable networks.
⚡
Infrastructure assumptions break down
Most AI products are designed for always-on cloud environments. Many businesses operate with tighter hardware budgets, unstable power, and real limits on what can stay connected all day.
🔒
Data control is not optional
When data is processed through cloud AI systems, retention, deletion, and governance are influenced by provider policies and legal obligations outside the organization’s direct control.

The Solution

AI that runs where your business runs.

Trosyn AI deploys on company-controlled infrastructure. Teams use it through a simple interface, while documents, prompts, and workflow context stay local. Teams doing high-volume document work often hit cloud AI rate limits. Local deployment removes those limits by shifting usage from vendor quotas to available hardware.
