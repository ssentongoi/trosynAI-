The Problem

African businesses can't use
the AI tools that exist.

Global AI platforms were built for always-on internet, Western regulations, and English workflows. African SMEs live in a different reality — and no one has built for it. Until now.

01
Connectivity is Unreliable
Only 1.2% of Africans have 5G access. 710 million people remain unconnected despite living in mobile broadband coverage areas. Cloud AI tools fail completely when the internet goes down.
21 internet shutdowns across 15 African countries in 2024 — costing businesses $7.69B globally. (Access Now / WEF, 2025)
02
Infrastructure is Unreliable
Frequent power outages, unstable internet, and strict data laws make always-on systems unreliable across the continent. Most global software assumes constant uptime conditions that simply don't exist here.
Cloud AI tools send data overseas by default — making them non-compliant across 39 of 55 African nations that now have active data protection laws.
03
Compliance is Mandatory
39 out of 55 African nations have enacted data protection laws. Nigeria and South Africa mandate local data hosting for financial and health data. Africa loses ~10% of GDP to cyberattacks annually.
Average breach cost: $4.88M (IBM, 2024). For most African SMEs, a single attack means permanent closure.

The Solution

100% offline. 100% on-premise.
Zero data leaves the building.

Trosyn AI is a modular AI platform that runs entirely on a local server inside the business. Users interact through a simple chat interface. All processing happens on-site. Nothing goes to the cloud.

Employment contracts, onboarding documents, payroll queries, leave management — compliant with local labor law.

Why it works where others don't
  • Runs on low-resource local servers using quantized LLMs (Gemma 3, Mistral 7B)
  • Adapted with African regulatory context, local languages, and business formats
  • Works during outages, shutdowns, and low-connectivity environments
  • Compliant by design — meets POPIA, NDPR, Kenya's Data Protection Act automatically
  • Multi-user collaboration — teams work together simultaneously inside the platform
  • Learns how each business works over time — improving memory and context awareness
  • Simple chat UI — no AI expertise required from staff
