Africa's businesses deserve AI that works in the real world — not a lab.

We are building the infrastructure layer that allows any African business — a logistics company in Kampala, a bank in Lagos, an NGO in Nairobi — to use powerful AI without sending their data offshore, without needing constant internet, and without paying Western enterprise prices.

Market Gap

Three forces are making cloud AI unworkable in Africa — simultaneously.

  • Connectivity CrisisOnly 1.2% of Africans have 5G. 21 internet shutdowns across 15 countries in 2024 cost businesses $7.69B. Cloud AI stops working the moment connectivity drops. (Access Now, 2025)
  • Unreliable InfrastructureFrequent power outages and unstable internet make always-on systems impractical. Global software assumes constant uptime conditions that don't exist in most African markets.
  • Data Sovereignty Laws39 of 55 African nations now have data protection laws. Nigeria mandates local hosting for financial and health data. South Africa's POPIA: fines up to 10% of global turnover. Cloud AI is non-compliant by design.
  • Existential Security RiskAfrica loses ~10% of GDP to cyberattacks annually. Average breach cost: $4.88M (IBM, 2024). For most SMEs, one breach means closure. Cloud-connected tools dramatically increase attack surface.

Solution

Offline-first AI. On your servers. Compliant by default.

Trosyn AI is a modular AI platform that runs entirely on a local server inside the business. Users interact via a simple chat interface. All processing stays on-site. Nothing goes to the cloud.

  • Runs on quantized LLMs (Gemma 3, Mistral 7B) — optimised for low-resource local servers
  • Trained with African regulatory context — local labor law, tax frameworks, compliance standards
  • Works during outages, shutdowns, and low-connectivity environments
  • Meets POPIA, NDPR, and Kenya's Data Protection Act automatically — no configuration needed
  • Multi-user collaboration — teams work together, platform learns workflows over time
  • Simple chat UI — no AI expertise required from non-technical staff

Risks & Mitigation

We've mapped them.
We've built around them.

Tech Risk
Benchmarking Gemma 3, Mistral 7B, and Llama 3 in quantized configurations. Speed/accuracy tradeoffs are acceptable for document generation — our use cases don't require real-time processing.
Adoption Risk
Starting with the highest-ROI, lowest-risk use cases — contract drafting and reporting — where time savings are immediate and measurable. Local pilot case studies are the primary sales tool.
Regulatory Risk
Trosyn already exceeds all current African data protection requirements. Tighter regulation is a tailwind for us, not a headwind — it eliminates cloud competitors from key sectors.
Competition Risk
Microsoft and Google are cloud-dependent by architecture. Rebuilding for true offline delivery would conflict with their core business model and take years. African regulatory knowledge is a further moat.
Talent Risk
Senior AI engineer hired remotely (global market), supplemented by local talent via iHub Nairobi and Kampala tech hub partnerships. Lean team structure keeps burn low.
